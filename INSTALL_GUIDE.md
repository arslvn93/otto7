# How to Install Otto

Otto installs in under a minute. There's no software to download, no folders to drag, no settings to fiddle with. If you can install an app on your phone, you can install Otto.

---

## Before you start

You need:

- **Claude Desktop** installed on your Mac or PC. If you don't have it yet, download it free at [claude.ai/download](https://claude.ai/download).
- A **Claude account** (free or paid both work).

That's it.

---

## Install in 4 steps

### Step 1 — Open Cowork

Open Claude Desktop. In the top of the window, click the **Cowork** tab. Cowork is the side of Claude built for getting real work done — it's where Otto lives.

### Step 2 — Open the plugin browser

In the left sidebar of Cowork, click **Customize**. Then click **Browse plugins**. A panel will open showing the plugins you can install.

### Step 3 — Add the Otto marketplace

At the top of the plugin browser, paste in this address:

```
https://github.com/arslvn93/otto
```

Press enter. Otto will appear in the list.

### Step 4 — Click Install

Find Otto in the list and click the **Install** button next to it. That's it. Otto is now ready.

---

## First time using Otto

Open a new Cowork conversation and just say **"hi"** — that's literally all you need to type.

The very first time you talk to Otto, it will pause and say something like:

> *"Before I can help with that, I need about two minutes to get to know you."*

Otto will then ask you a few quick questions in plain English:

- Your name, brokerage, phone, and email
- What areas you work and what you specialize in
- Your social media handles (optional)
- How you want Otto to sound — Professional, Warm, Casual, Luxury, or "match my style"
- How you sign off your emails
- Anything personal you want Otto to weave into your content (your dog, the team you sponsor, the fact that you never use exclamation marks, etc.)

That whole conversation takes about two minutes. Once you're done, Otto saves your profile and creates an **Otto Workspace** folder for you. You only do this once. Otto remembers everything forever after.

## How to actually use Otto

Otto is built around **packages** — entire workflows for the four stages of a real estate transaction. Every time you start a fresh conversation with Otto, you'll see a simple menu with these options:

- **Start a Listing package** — new property going to market
- **Start a Buyer package** — new buyer client onboarding
- **Start an Under Contract package** — deal accepted, working through conditions
- **Start a Post-Close & Nurture package** — deal closed, starting nurture sequence
- **Other** — one-off requests (single email, social post, listing description, script, etc.)

Just click the option you want.

### What happens when you pick a package

Let's say you click **Listing package**. Otto will:

1. Ask you the property address (and look up whether you've already worked on it)
2. Ask one batch of questions covering everything it needs — property basics, seller info, marketing details, showing logistics, target buyer, etc.
3. Generate **all 9 listing-side deliverables in one go**:
   - Seller welcome email
   - Seller onboarding survey
   - Pre-listing checklist
   - Go-to-market prep email
   - MLS listing description
   - Weekly showing feedback template
   - Price reduction email
   - Offer presentation email
   - Just-sold + next steps email
4. Save every file into a numbered subfolder inside that listing's folder so it sorts in transaction order
5. Send you back a single summary with the file paths so you can open the folder and grab whatever you need

That's it. You answered one round of questions and you have a full listing-side workflow ready to send.

### The other packages work the same way

- **Buyer package** generates 6 buy-side deliverables (consultation, welcome, onboarding survey, listing alert template, offer process review, offer prep questionnaire)
- **Under Contract package** generates the transaction checklist and purchaser visit email
- **Post-Close & Nurture package** generates 5 nurture-stage items (closing congrats, referral request, annual check-in, birthday message, occasion reminders calendar)

### One-off requests

Anything not in a package — a single email, a social post, a script for a tough conversation — just pick "Other" or type the request in plain English:

- *"Write a just-listed Instagram post for 742 Maple Drive."*
- *"My buyer wants to wait for interest rates to drop. Give me talking points."*
- *"Draft a price reduction email — 45 days on market, recommending a $20K drop."*
- *"Roleplay a FSBO call with me."*

Otto picks the right template, writes the content in your voice, and saves it to the right folder.

## Where your work lives

Otto saves everything into an **Otto Workspace** folder on your computer. Inside it you'll find:

- **Listings/** — every listing you've worked on, with the full transaction history nested inside
- **Buyers/** — every buyer client, with their full journey from consultation to closing
- **Post-Close/** — your nurture pipeline for past clients
- **Open Houses/**, **Offers/**, **Marketing/**, **Prospecting/** — for things that don't fit the main flows

When you walk away from Otto and come back next week, your work is right where you left it.

## Updating your profile

If anything about you or your business changes, just tell Otto in plain English:

- *"Update my profile — my new phone is (416) 555-0123."*
- *"I switched brokerages. I'm now at Compass."*
- *"Change my email sign-off to 'Talk soon, Jess.'"*

Otto will rewrite the relevant section and confirm it. No file editing needed.

## Troubleshooting

**"Otto isn't showing up after I clicked Install."**
Quit Claude Desktop completely (right-click the icon in your dock or system tray and choose Quit), then reopen it. Otto should appear.

**"Otto didn't show me the menu when I said hi."**
Make sure you're starting a brand new conversation. The menu shows up at the start of fresh conversations, not in the middle of one.

**"Otto didn't ask me the onboarding questions."**
Onboarding only triggers on the very first message of your very first session ever. If you've already onboarded, Otto skips straight to the menu.

**"Otto wrote something in the wrong tone."**
Tell it. Say *"that's too formal — make it warmer"* or *"shorter, more punchy."* Otto adjusts on the fly. If you want a permanent change, say *"update my profile — change my tone to Warm & Approachable."*

**"I want to start over with onboarding."**
Just say *"Otto, let's start over with onboarding"* and it will rerun the full setup conversation.

**"I want to skip the menu and just ask for one thing."**
Just type your request directly — *"draft a price reduction email for 742 Maple Drive"* — and Otto will skip the menu and handle it.
