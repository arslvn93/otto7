# Just Sold — Social Media Post Templates

**Template Type:** Social media — Closed deal celebration
**Tone:** Celebratory, grateful, and subtly impressive. This post does double duty: it celebrates the client AND positions the agent as someone who gets results. Never brag — let the facts speak.
**When to use:** Within 1-3 days of closing. Always get client permission before posting, especially if tagging them or sharing personal details.

---

## Required Details

- Property address (or just neighborhood if the client prefers privacy)
- Sale price (optional — some clients and agents prefer not to share)
- Over/under asking (if it tells a good story)
- Days on market
- Whether the agent represented the buyer or seller
- Client story (first-time buyer, upsizing, downsizing, relocation, investor) — only share what the client is comfortable with
- Any notable details (multiple offers, over asking, record price, tough negotiation, emotional moment)
- Client permission to post and tag

## Tone Notes for Claude

- Always ask if the client gave permission to post. Never assume.
- The client's story is the heart of the post. If the agent provides a good personal detail (first home, relocated for a job, found the perfect forever home after a long search), lead with it.
- Stats are supporting evidence, not the headline. "Sold in 6 days for $18K over asking" is impressive, but "watching them pick out bedrooms" is memorable.
- Never reveal anything the client wouldn't want public — financial struggles, divorce, estate sale circumstances, etc.
- The subtle positioning at the end ("thinking about buying?") should feel earned, not forced. You just proved you get results — let that do the selling.
- If the deal was tough (long DOM, price reductions, difficult negotiation), don't pretend it was easy. An honest "this wasn't straightforward" story is more compelling than a highlight reel.
- For seller-side posts, focus on the strategy and result. For buyer-side posts, focus on the human story.
