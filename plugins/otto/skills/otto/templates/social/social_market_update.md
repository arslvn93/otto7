# Market Update — Social Media Post Templates

**Template Type:** Social media — Monthly or quarterly market stats post
**Tone:** Authoritative but accessible. This is the agent's chance to position themselves as the local market expert. The data should feel clear and useful, not dry or intimidating.
**When to use:** Monthly or quarterly. Best posted in the first week of the month using the previous month's data. Consistency matters more than frequency.

---

## Required Details

- Market area (city, neighborhood, zip code, or county)
- Time period (month, quarter, year-over-year)
- Key stats the agent wants to highlight (provide at least 3):
  - Median or average sale price (and trend direction)
  - Number of homes sold
  - Average days on market
  - Inventory level (months of supply)
  - List-to-sale price ratio
  - New listings count
  - Interest rate context (optional but useful)
- The agent's interpretation — what does this mean for buyers and sellers?

**Important:** All stats must come from the agent. Claude should NEVER fabricate, estimate, or look up market data. If the agent doesn't provide numbers, ask for them.

## Tone Notes for Claude

- Stats without interpretation are useless. The agent's analysis is what separates this from a Zillow chart. Always push for "what does this mean."
- Never fabricate market data. If the agent doesn't provide the numbers, ask: "What are the key stats for your market this month? I need at least median price, DOM, and inventory to make a strong post."
- Avoid doomer or hype language. Don't say "the market is crashing" or "prices are exploding." Use measured language: "prices have softened," "demand remains strong," "inventory is building."
- The call to action should always offer something free and no-pressure: a home valuation, a neighborhood analysis, or just a conversation.
- Consistency is key — if the agent commits to monthly updates, help them maintain the format so followers recognize the series.
- Year-over-year comparisons are more meaningful than month-over-month. Monthly fluctuations can be misleading.
