# Just Listed — Social Media Post Templates

**Template Type:** Social media — New listing announcement
**Tone:** Excited but not hypey. Let the property sell itself with great details and a clear call to action. Sound like a knowledgeable local agent, not a used car ad.
**When to use:** The day the listing goes live on MLS, or the day before as a "coming soon" teaser.

---

## Required Details

- Property address (or neighborhood if the agent prefers not to share the full address publicly)
- Beds / Baths / Square footage
- List price
- 2-3 standout features
- Open house date/time (if scheduled)
- Link to listing (MLS, agent website, or portal)
- Photo or video — the agent will handle this, but remind them: the first photo is everything on social

## LinkedIn Post

**Format:** Professional tone. Less emoji. Position the listing as a market insight or professional milestone, not just an ad.

---

New listing: [Property Address]

[Opening line that frames the listing in a market context — why this property matters, what it says about the neighborhood, or what makes it unusual.]

[Brief property summary — 2-3 sentences max. Focus on quality, not hype.]

---

## Tone Notes for Claude

- Every post should feel native to its platform. Instagram is visual-first with clean formatting. Facebook is conversational and shareable. LinkedIn is professional and insight-driven.
- Never use ALL CAPS for emphasis (except "NEW LISTING" on Facebook, which is convention).
- Emoji: use sparingly. 2-3 max on Instagram, 2-4 on Facebook, 0-1 on LinkedIn.
- Always include a call to action — what should the reader DO? (DM, comment, tag, click, show up to the open house.)
- Hashtags are Instagram-only. Don't use them on Facebook or LinkedIn.
- If the agent provides the property details but no standout features, ask: "What's the one thing that makes this home special? What did you notice first when you walked in?"
