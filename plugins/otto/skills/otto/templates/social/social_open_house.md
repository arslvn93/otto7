# Open House — Social Media Post Templates

**Template Type:** Social media — Open house promotion
**Tone:** Inviting and informative. The goal is to get people to show up — both potential buyers AND nosy neighbors (who refer friends and family). Make it easy: when, where, and why it's worth their time.
**When to use:** Post 2-3 days before the open house, then again the morning of. Two touchpoints doubles attendance.

---

## Required Details

- Property address
- Open house date and time
- Beds / Baths / Square footage
- List price
- 2-3 standout features (what will make someone stop scrolling and put it on their calendar)
- Link to listing
- Photo — ideally the best exterior or the hero room (kitchen, living area, backyard)

## Tone Notes for Claude

- The #1 goal is getting people to show up. Make it frictionless: "no appointment needed" should appear in every version.
- Lead with the experiential hook — what will they see, feel, or notice when they walk in? Don't just repeat the listing stats.
- The "bring your friends / share this" CTA is intentional. Open houses generate leads for the agent, not just offers on this property. Nosy neighbors are future clients.
- Morning-of Instagram Stories are casual and unpolished on purpose. Authenticity outperforms production value here.
- If the agent doesn't provide standout features, ask: "What's the one thing that's going to surprise people when they walk in?"
- Don't over-promise. If it's a modest home, lean into value and location. Don't try to make a 1,200 sq ft ranch sound like a mansion.
- Include the price. Agents sometimes want to withhold it to drive curiosity — in practice, buyers scroll past listings without prices.
