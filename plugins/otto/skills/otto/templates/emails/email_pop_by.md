# Pop-By Email

**Template Type:** Email — short, seasonal, relationship-maintenance note that accompanies (or replaces) a small gift drop-off
**Tone:** Casual, warm, zero pressure. This is the "just thinking of you" touch.
**When to use:** Seasonally (1–4x per year). Common anchors: spring/fall market kickoff, summer, Thanksgiving, holidays, or any time you're in their neighborhood.

---

## Required Details

- Client first name
- Season or occasion
- Whether there's an actual pop-by gift being dropped off (pie, pumpkin, plant, etc.) or if it's email-only

---

## Email Template

**Subject line options by season:**
- Spring: "Quick spring hello from [Agent First Name]"
- Summer: "Summer check-in"
- Fall: "A little something for fall"
- Holidays: "Happy holidays from [Agent First Name]"
- Generic: "Just thinking of you"

---

**Variant A — With a pop-by drop-off**

Hi [First Name],

Hope you and [partner / the family / the place] are doing well.

I was in your neighborhood [today / this week] and dropped off a little [pumpkin / pie / bouquet / holiday card / seedling / bag of apples / cinnamon broom] on your front porch. No occasion — just wanted to say hi the old-fashioned way.

[One optional personal touch — "Hope that kitchen renovation is going well" or "Still the best cup of coffee I've ever had on your back deck" or "Can't believe it's almost been [X] years in the place."]

No need to reply. If I catch you outside next time I'm by, I'll stop for a minute.

Take care,

[Agent First Name]

---

**Variant B — Email only, no pop-by**

Hi [First Name],

Quick note — I was thinking about you and [property / neighborhood / specific thing they mentioned] this week and wanted to say hello.

[One personal line — "Hope the new deck held up through the winter" / "Can't wait to see how the garden looks this spring" / "Still can't get over that kitchen."]

No ask, no update, nothing you need to reply to. Just checking in the way good friends do.

Hope you're well.

[Agent First Name]

---

## Tone Notes for Claude

- **Never** include a business ask, market update, or CTA. This is the ONE email in the nurture sequence that is 100% relationship.
- Keep it under 100 words
- Must feel human — if the agent's `my_profile.md` mentions a personal quirk ("I never use exclamation marks" or "I always sign off with 'chat soon'"), honor it
- The power of the pop-by comes from the specificity — a generic "thinking of you" is forgettable. One concrete, specific detail makes it memorable.
