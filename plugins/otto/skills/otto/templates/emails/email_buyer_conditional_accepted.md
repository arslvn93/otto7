# Conditional Offer Accepted — Congrats + Next Steps (Buyer)

**Template Type:** Email — sent the moment a buyer's conditional offer is accepted
**Tone:** Celebratory, but immediately operational. They're excited AND nervous. Both are valid.
**When to use:** Within 1 hour of acceptance.

---

## Required Details

- Buyer name(s)
- Property address
- Accepted price
- Closing date
- List of conditions
- Waiver date for each condition
- Deposit amount + deadline

---

## Email Template

**Subject line options:**
- We got it — [Property Address] is yours (conditionally)
- Offer accepted on [Property Address] — here's what happens next
- Congratulations — let's walk through the next [X] days

---

**Email body:**

Hi [Buyer Name(s)],

Congratulations — **[Property Address] is yours**, pending conditions, at **$[Accepted Price]**. Take a second to enjoy that. Seriously — it's a big moment and I don't want us to blow past it.

Okay. Now let me walk you through the next few days so nothing catches you off guard.

**You are currently "under contract but conditional."**

That means the deal is accepted but not yet final. We have a short window to complete a few things, and if everything checks out, we waive the conditions and the deal goes **firm**. Until then, technically either side can walk if a condition isn't met — but that's rare when we've done our homework.

**Here are your conditions and when each needs to be waived:**

- [Condition 1 — e.g., Financing] — waive by **[date]**
- [Condition 2 — e.g., Home inspection] — waive by **[date]**
- [Condition 3 — e.g., Status certificate] — waive by **[date]**

**What happens between now and then**

**Deposit — within [24 hours / X business days]**
Your deposit of **$[amount]** needs to be delivered [to my brokerage / to the listing brokerage] in certified funds or wire. I'll send you the exact delivery instructions in a separate email. This is non-negotiable — if the deposit is late, the deal can collapse.

**Home inspection — I'll book it**
I'll coordinate the inspection with an inspector I trust. You should plan to attend at least the last 30 minutes so the inspector can walk you through what they found. Expect it to last 2–3 hours.

**Financing — call your lender today**
Contact [Lender Name] or your mortgage broker and let them know the offer was accepted. They'll need the accepted agreement, which I'll forward to them directly. They will move fast to finalize your approval before the waiver date.

**Status certificate (condo only)**
I'll order the status certificate from the listing agent. It takes [X] business days to arrive. Once it's here, I'll send it to your lawyer to review — their feedback determines whether we waive this condition.

**Your lawyer**
If you don't have a real estate lawyer yet, you need one **now**. I can recommend two or three if you want. They need the agreement today or tomorrow.

---

**Critically important during this period**

- **Do not make any big financial moves.** No new credit cards, no car loans, no furniture financing. Your pre-approval can get pulled if your credit profile changes.
- **Do not quit your job or switch jobs.** Same reason.
- **Do not send any money to anyone other than the deposit destination.** Fraud targeting buyers is real — if you get an email asking you to wire money somewhere, call me before doing anything.
- **Stay reachable.** I'll need quick decisions from you in the next few days.

**What happens after we waive conditions**

Once all conditions are waived, the deal is **firm** — legally binding, no more escape hatches. At that point, your lawyer takes the lead on the closing process, and I'll send you a separate email walking you through what happens between firm and closing day.

---

Any questions — any questions at all, even ones that feel dumb — call or text. This is exactly the moment where I want to hear from you.

Congratulations again. We're going to get this across the finish line.

[Agent Sign-Off]

[Signature Block]

---

## Tone Notes for Claude

- Equal parts celebration and operational clarity
- The warnings about finances and fraud are critical — do not skip or soften them
- Under 700 words (long because it has to be)
- Adjust condition list to match what the agent provides in intake
