# Offer Presentation Email

**Template Type:** Email — Presenting a received offer to the seller
**Tone:** Clear, organized, and neutral. The agent's job here is to present the facts and help the seller evaluate — not to push them toward accepting or rejecting.
**When to use:** When an offer is received and the agent needs to present it to the seller, especially when an in-person meeting isn't immediately possible.

---

## Required Details

- Seller name(s)
- Property address
- List price
- Offer price
- Buyer name(s)
- Buyer's agent name and brokerage
- Pre-approval or proof of funds status
- Down payment amount and percentage
- Loan type (conventional, FHA, VA, cash)
- Earnest money deposit amount
- Contingencies (inspection, financing, appraisal, sale of buyer's home)
- Proposed closing date
- Any special terms (seller concessions, rent-back, personal property requests, escalation clause)
- Offer expiration date/time if applicable

---

## Email Template

**Subject line options:**
- We have an offer on [Property Address]
- Offer received — [Property Address]
- [Property Address] — offer details inside

---

Hi [Seller Name(s)],

Great news — we've received an offer on [Property Address]. I've reviewed the full package and want to walk you through the key terms so you can start thinking about how you'd like to respond.

**Offer Summary:**

- **Offer Price:** $[Offer Price] [if different from list: "(your list price is $[List Price])"]
- **Buyer:** [Buyer Name(s)]
- **Buyer's Agent:** [Agent Name], [Brokerage]
- **Financing:** [Loan type — Conventional / FHA / VA / Cash] with [down payment %] down ($[down payment amount])
- **Pre-Approval:** [Status — "Verified pre-approval letter included" or "Proof of funds provided" or "Pre-qualification letter only — I'd recommend requesting a full pre-approval"]
- **Earnest Money:** $[EMD Amount]
- **Proposed Closing Date:** [Date] ([X] days from acceptance)

**Contingencies:**
- Inspection: [Yes — X days / Waived]
- Financing: [Yes — X days / Waived / N/A if cash]
- Appraisal: [Yes / Waived / Gap coverage offered up to $X]
- Sale of Buyer's Home: [Yes — details / No]

**Other Terms:**
[List any special requests — seller concessions, rent-back period, personal property inclusions, escalation clause details, home warranty request, etc. If none: "No additional special terms."]

[If offer has an expiration: "**This offer expires at [time] on [date].**"]

**My Initial Take:**

[2-3 sentences of professional assessment. Examples:]
- "This is a strong offer — the buyer is well-qualified, the terms are clean, and the timeline works. The price is [at/near/below] asking, and the contingencies are standard."
- "The offer is below asking, but the buyer is pre-approved and offering a quick close with minimal contingencies. There's room to counter on price while keeping the deal together."
- "The financing type (FHA/VA) means the appraisal requirements are stricter, which is worth discussing. The buyer's overall package is solid otherwise."
- "The offer includes a sale contingency on the buyer's home, which adds risk. I'd want to understand where their home stands before we respond."

**Our Options:**

1. **Accept** the offer as written
2. **Counter** on price, terms, or both
3. **Reject** and continue marketing

I'd recommend we schedule a quick call to discuss. I can walk you through how this compares to recent sales and what I think the best strategy is, given where we are in the market.

Are you available [today/tomorrow] at [time]? [Or: "What time works best for you?"]

[Agent Name]
[Phone]
[Email]

## Tone Notes for Claude

- Present the offer neutrally first, then give a professional assessment. Don't lead with your opinion before they've seen the terms.
- The "My Initial Take" section should be honest. If the offer is weak, say so diplomatically. If it's strong, say that too. Sellers trust agents who call it straight.
- Always end with "let's talk" — an offer response is a conversation, not a snap decision.
- If there are multiple offers, adjust the framing: "We've received [X] offers and I want to present each one clearly so we can compare." Then use a comparison format with the same categories for each offer.
- Never pressure the seller to accept. Never say "I think we should take this." Say "here's what I'd consider" or "here's how I see the options."
- If any terms are unusual or risky, flag them clearly. The seller should never be surprised by something you didn't mention.

## Variant — Multiple Offers

If presenting more than one offer, add this section after the summary:

**Offer Comparison:**

| Term | Offer A | Offer B | Offer C |
|------|---------|---------|---------|
| Price | $X | $X | $X |
| Financing | Conv. 20% down | FHA 3.5% down | Cash |
| Earnest Money | $X | $X | $X |
| Contingencies | Inspection + Appraisal | Inspection + Financing + Appraisal | None |
| Closing Date | [Date] | [Date] | [Date] |
| Special Terms | None | $5K seller concession | 30-day rent-back |
| Pre-Approval | Verified | Pre-qual only | Proof of funds |

"I'll share my assessment of each when we talk, but at a glance, [brief observation — e.g., 'Offer C is the cleanest but Offer A has the highest price. The trade-off is worth discussing.']"
