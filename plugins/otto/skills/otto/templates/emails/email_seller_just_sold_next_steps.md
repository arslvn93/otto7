# Just Sold + Next Steps Email (Seller)

**Template Type:** Email — sent to seller the day their offer is accepted and firm
**Tone:** Celebratory but professional, and immediately pivots to what happens next.
**When to use:** Same day as firm acceptance (or condition waiver if conditional).

---

## Required Details

- Seller name(s)
- Property address
- Sale price
- Closing date
- Buyer's lawyer name (if known)
- Seller's lawyer name
- Deposit status

---

## Email Template

**Subject line options:**
- It's official — [Property Address] is sold
- We did it — here's what happens between now and closing
- Congratulations on the sale of [Property Address]

---

**Email body:**

Hi [Seller Name(s)],

[Property Address] is officially sold at **$[Sale Price]**, closing **[Closing Date]**. Congratulations — you earned this.

Now let me walk you through what happens between now and closing day so you know exactly what to expect.

**This week**

- I'll forward the full signed agreement to your lawyer, [Seller Lawyer Name]. If you haven't already connected with them, expect a call or email from their office within the next few days to set up your file.
- The buyer's deposit of **$[Deposit Amount]** will be delivered [to my brokerage / to your lawyer's trust] within [24 hours / 3 business days / as specified].
- I'll coordinate with the buyer's agent on any remaining paperwork or signatures.

**Between now and closing**

- Keep your home insured and utilities on until closing day — this is non-negotiable.
- Any damage or change to the property between now and closing (even accidental) can cause problems. Treat the home like it's still yours but also like it's someone else's — because legally, both are about to be true.
- If the buyer requested a pre-closing visit (24–48 hours before closing), I'll coordinate it with you.
- Start thinking about utilities transfer (hydro, gas, water, internet, cable) — you'll cancel or transfer those effective closing day.
- Change of address: Canada Post / USPS, driver's license, banks, subscriptions. You can start this any time.

**Closing day**

- Your lawyer handles the paperwork and fund transfer — you don't need to be physically present unless they tell you otherwise.
- Keys are typically exchanged through the lawyers. I'll coordinate the handoff.
- Once the deal closes, I'll confirm with you directly. Expect a text from me that afternoon.

**After closing**

- I'll send a short survey (totally optional) so I can keep improving how I serve clients.
- If you know anyone thinking of buying or selling, please think of me. Referrals are the best compliment you can give me, and I treat every referral like family.
- I'm not disappearing after closing — I'll check in throughout the year.

**A few things to do this week:**

1. Confirm your lawyer is in the loop (I'll forward the paperwork today)
2. Make sure home insurance stays active until [Closing Date]
3. Start the mental list of what you need to move, cancel, and transfer

Really, truly congratulations. This was a team effort and you did your part beautifully. Any questions between now and closing — text, call, or email any time.

[Agent Sign-Off]

[Signature Block]

---

## Tone Notes for Claude

- 60% operational, 40% celebratory — don't bury the next steps under sentiment
- Use "we" for shared achievement, "you" for the seller's tasks
- Under 500 words
- Never promise what the lawyer will or won't do — defer to them for legal/financial specifics
