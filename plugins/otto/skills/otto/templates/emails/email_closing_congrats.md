# Closing Congratulations Email

**Template Type:** Email — Sent to the client after a successful closing
**Tone:** Genuinely celebratory and warm. This is the emotional peak of the relationship. The agent should feel like a trusted friend congratulating them, not a service provider wrapping up a project.
**When to use:** Day of closing or the morning after.

---

## Required Details

- Client name(s)
- Property address
- Whether they're a buyer or seller (tone and content differ)
- Any personal touches from the relationship (first-time buyer, long search, tough negotiation, emotional moment at closing)

---

## Template A — Buyer Closing

**Subject line options:**
- Congratulations, homeowner!
- The keys are yours — [Property Address]
- You did it, [Buyer Name]!

---

Hi [Buyer Name(s)],

You're officially homeowners. I'm so happy for you.

[Personal touch — reference something specific from the journey. Examples:]
- "I know this search took longer than either of us expected, but I hope you agree — it was worth the wait. This home is perfect for you."
- "Watching you walk through [Property Address] for the first time and seeing your face — I knew that was the one. Glad we moved fast."
- "From that first meeting where you weren't sure you could even afford to buy, to today holding the keys to your own home — you should be really proud."

**A few things to take care of this week:**

- **Utilities:** Transfer electric, gas, water, internet, and trash service into your name. Your title company or I can provide a list of local providers if you need it.
- **Address change:** Update your address with the post office (usps.com), your bank, employer, insurance, subscriptions, and anywhere else that mails you stuff.
- **Locks:** Consider rekeying or changing the locks. You don't know how many copies of the old keys are floating around.
- **Home warranty:** [If applicable: "Your home warranty is active through [Company]. Here's the claim number: [number]. Keep it handy in case anything comes up in the first year."]
- **Homestead exemption:** If this is your primary residence, file for your homestead exemption with the county. It can significantly reduce your property taxes. [Add local details if known.]

**One more thing:**

If you know anyone — friends, family, coworkers — who's thinking about buying or selling, I'd be honored if you'd pass along my name. Referrals are the best compliment I can receive, and I promise I'll take care of them the same way I took care of you.

Enjoy your first night in your new home. You earned it.

[Agent Name]
[Phone]
[Email]

## Template B — Seller Closing

**Subject line options:**
- Congratulations — [Property Address] is officially sold
- You're done — and you crushed it
- Sold! Here's to what's next, [Seller Name]

---

Hi [Seller Name(s)],

It's done — [Property Address] has officially closed, and you're free. Congratulations.

[Personal touch — reference something specific. Examples:]
- "From our first meeting to today, you trusted the process and it paid off. $[sale price] is a great result."
- "I know the price reduction conversation wasn't easy, but the strategy worked exactly as we discussed — and you ended up in a stronger position because of it."
- "This was a complicated one with [specific challenge], and I'm glad we got through it together."

**A few things to wrap up:**

- **Proceeds:** Your net proceeds will be wired to your account, typically within 1-2 business days. Your title company can confirm the timeline.
- **Tax records:** Keep your closing documents in a safe place. Your CPA will want the settlement statement at tax time.
- **Mail forwarding:** Set up mail forwarding through USPS if you haven't already.
- **Cancel services:** Remember to cancel or transfer utilities, lawn care, pest control, security monitoring, and any other services tied to the property.

**Looking ahead:**

[If they're buying: "Now let's go find your next home. I'll be in touch about [next steps]."]
[If they're relocating: "I hope the move to [new city] goes smoothly. If you need an agent recommendation in your new area, I have connections nationwide — just say the word."]
[If they're done: "If you ever need anything real estate related — or just want to grab coffee — don't be a stranger."]

And if anyone you know is thinking about buying or selling, I'd love the chance to help them. Your referral means the world to me.

Thank you for trusting me with this. It was a pleasure.

[Agent Name]
[Phone]
[Email]

---

## Tone Notes for Claude

- This is not a transactional email. It's a relationship email. Make it feel personal.
- The personal touch paragraph is non-negotiable. If the agent doesn't provide a specific detail, ask for one. Generic congratulations feel hollow.
- The practical next-steps section adds real value and reminds the client that the agent is helpful beyond just the transaction.
- The referral ask should feel natural, not forced. One mention, positioned as a compliment, at the end.
- Keep it under 300 words. They just went through an emotionally exhausting process — respect their attention.
