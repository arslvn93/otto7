# Price Reduction Email

**Template Type:** Email — Recommending a price adjustment to the seller
**Tone:** Empathetic but direct. Data-driven, not apologetic. The agent is doing their job by being honest — this isn't a failure, it's a market response.
**When to use:** When a listing has been on market without acceptable offers and the agent believes a price adjustment is needed. Typically after 2-4 weeks depending on the market.

---

## Required Details

- Seller name(s)
- Property address
- Current list price
- Days on market
- Number of showings
- Showing feedback themes (if any)
- Recommended new price and rationale
- Recent comparable sales or price changes in the area
- Any new competition (similar listings that have come on market)

---

## Email Template

**Subject line options:**
- Market update on [Property Address] — let's talk strategy
- [Property Address] — my recommendation after [X] days
- Checking in with data on [Property Address]

---

Hi [Seller Name(s)],

I want to give you an honest update on where we stand with [Property Address] after [X] days on the market.

**Here's what the data is telling us:**

- We've had [X] showings so far.
- [If feedback exists: "The consistent feedback from agents and buyers has been [theme — e.g., 'they love the kitchen but feel the price is high relative to comparable options,' or 'most buyers are comparing us to [competing property] which is listed at $X.'"]
- [If no/low showings: "The showing activity has been lower than I'd expect for this area and price point, which typically signals that buyers are scrolling past us online in favor of homes they perceive as better value."]
- [New competition note if applicable: "Since we listed, [X] similar homes have come on the market in the $[range], giving buyers more options in our category."]

**What I'm recommending:**

I believe a price adjustment to **$[New Price]** will reposition us competitively and generate the kind of activity that leads to strong offers. Here's why that number:

[2-3 sentences of comp-based rationale. Example: "The most recent comparable sale was [address] at $[price], which was similar in size and condition. The homes currently getting offers in the neighborhood are priced between $[range]. At $[new price], we'd be positioned as one of the best values in that bracket, which is where multiple-offer situations happen."]

**What this is NOT:**

This isn't about giving your home away or admitting something is wrong with it. Your home is [genuine positive comment]. The market is just telling us that at the current price, buyers have other options they're choosing first. A strategic adjustment now — while we still have momentum — is much more effective than a series of small reductions over time.

**What happens next:**

If you're open to the adjustment, I can update the price immediately. A price change triggers a re-notification to every buyer and agent who viewed the listing, so it effectively relaunches us to the market.

I'd love to talk this through — are you available for a quick call [today/tomorrow]? I want to walk you through the comps and answer any questions.

[Agent Name]
[Phone]
[Email]

## Tone Notes for Claude

- This is one of the hardest emails an agent sends. The tone has to be empathetic AND direct. Don't bury the recommendation in caveats.
- Lead with data, not opinion. "The market is telling us" is stronger than "I think we should."
- The "What this is NOT" section is essential. Sellers take price reductions personally. Reframe it as strategy, not failure.
- Never blame the seller's home condition, staging, or decisions. Focus on market positioning.
- If the agent doesn't provide comp data or showing feedback, ask for it. This email doesn't work without specifics.
- Always end with a conversation, not a demand. "Are you available to talk" is better than "let me know what you want to do."
- Keep it under 350 words. Dense enough to be convincing, short enough that they read the whole thing.

---

## Variant — Second Price Reduction

If this is the second reduction, the tone shifts to more urgent but still respectful:

**Additional talking points:**
- "We've now been on market for [X] days. In our area, the average home sells in [Y] days. The longer we sit, the more buyers wonder what's wrong — even though the answer is simply pricing."
- "I'd rather have an honest conversation now than watch the listing go stale. Homes that reduce strategically early almost always net more than homes that chase the market down over months."
- Reference the first reduction and its result: "The adjustment to $[first reduction price] generated [X more showings / some interest / didn't change the activity], which confirms we need to get more aggressive to reach the right buyer pool."
