# Buyer Welcome Email

**Template Type:** Email — First contact after signing a buyer representation agreement
**Tone:** Warm, reassuring, and organized. Many buyers (especially first-timers) are overwhelmed. This email should make them feel like they're in good hands.
**When to use:** Within 24 hours of signing the buyer representation agreement or after the first buyer consultation.

---

## Required Details

- Buyer name(s)
- Pre-approval status (approved, in progress, not started)
- Lender name if applicable
- Target area(s) / neighborhoods
- Budget range
- Approximate timeline (when they want to be moved in)
- Any specific search criteria discussed (beds, baths, must-haves, dealbreakers)

---

## Email Template

**Subject line options:**
- Let's find your home — here's how we'll do it
- Welcome aboard — your home search game plan
- Next steps for your home search, [Buyer Name]

---

Hi [Buyer Name(s)],

I'm really looking forward to working with you on this. Buying a home is a big deal, and I want to make sure the process feels clear and manageable from start to finish.

Here's a quick overview of how we'll work together and what to expect.

**Where we start:**

[If pre-approved: "You're already pre-approved through [Lender] for up to $[amount], which puts us in a strong position. Sellers take pre-approved buyers seriously, and it means we can move fast when we find the right home."]

[If not yet pre-approved: "The first step is getting you pre-approved with a lender. This tells us exactly what you can comfortably afford and shows sellers you're a serious buyer. I'd recommend reaching out to [Lender recommendation or "a lender — I can suggest a few great options if you'd like"] this week so we're ready to write an offer when we find the one."]

**How the search works:**

- I'll set you up on an automated search through the MLS based on what we discussed: [brief summary — e.g., "3+ beds, 2+ baths, under $450K, in the Riverside or Oakwood areas"]. You'll get new listings emailed the moment they hit the market.
- When you see something you like, send it my way and I'll dig into the details — history, price context, neighborhood, anything that doesn't show up in the listing photos.
- I'll also be proactively looking for you. Some of the best opportunities are ones I'll flag before you see them on Zillow.
- When we schedule showings, I'll send you a route and any relevant notes on each home beforehand so your time is well spent.

**When we find the one:**

- I'll pull comparable sales to make sure we're offering the right price — not overpaying and not lowballing ourselves out of contention.
- I'll write the offer, walk you through every line, and handle all negotiation with the listing agent.
- Once we're under contract, I'll manage the entire process — inspection, appraisal, lender coordination, title, and closing — and keep you updated at every step.

**A few tips as we get started:**

- Don't apply for new credit cards, make large purchases, or change jobs during this process. All of those can affect your loan approval.
- Keep your phone handy. In a competitive market, speed matters. I'll try to get us into new listings as quickly as possible.
- No question is too small. Text, call, or email me anytime. I'd rather explain something twice than have you wonder.

[Personal touch — e.g., "Based on what you described, I think we're going to find some really strong options in [area]. The market there has [brief positive context]."]

Let's do this.

[Agent Name]

---

## Tone Notes for Claude

- First-time buyers need more reassurance and process explanation. Experienced buyers need less hand-holding — shorten the "how the search works" and "when we find the one" sections.
- The credit/job tip is important and often overlooked. Include it every time.
- Always personalize with at least one specific reference to their search criteria or situation.
- Keep it under 400 words. Excited buyers still don't want to read a novel.
- Never promise they'll find something quickly or at a specific price. Promise a process and your commitment.

---

## Variant — Relocating Buyer

Add this section if the buyer is moving from out of town:

"Since you're relocating from [City], I know this adds a layer of complexity. Here's how we'll handle it:

- I'll do video walkthroughs of homes you can't see in person so you get an honest, real-time look — not just listing photos.
- I'll give you neighborhood context that you can't Google — where traffic gets bad, which streets are quieter, where people actually shop and eat.
- We can structure your search into focused trips so when you're in town, every hour counts.
- If you need to make an offer remotely, we can do everything digitally — e-signatures, virtual walkthroughs, video calls."
