# Birthday Message — Text + Email Variants

**Template Type:** Short message — text and email variants
**Tone:** Warm, genuine, NOT a sales pitch. Nothing kills a nurture relationship faster than a birthday message that ends with "also, know anyone looking to sell?"
**When to use:** On the client's birthday. Pick ONE channel — don't send both.

---

## Required Details

- Client first name
- Closing / work history with the agent (a specific detail to reference is ideal)

---

## Text Variants (pick one)

**Short & warm:**
> Happy birthday, [First Name]! Hope you're being spoiled today. 🎂

**With a callback to the home:**
> Happy birthday, [First Name]! Hope you're celebrating in that beautiful [kitchen / backyard / living room] you love. 🎉

**Very short (for busy clients):**
> Happy birthday, [First Name]. Hope today's a good one.

**With a tiny personal touch:**
> Happy birthday, [First Name]! Thinking of you and [partner's name / family] today. Hope it's a great one. 🎂

---

## Email Variant

**Subject line:** Happy birthday, [First Name] 🎂

---

**Email body:**

Hi [First Name],

Just a quick note to say happy birthday. Hope today's being kind to you — good coffee, good food, and hopefully zero work email.

[One optional personal line — e.g., "Can't believe it's already been [X] years since we closed on [address]." or "Thinking of you and [partner/family] today."]

No agenda, no ask. Just wanted you to know you crossed my mind today.

Have a great one.

[Agent First Name]

---

## Tone Notes for Claude

- **NEVER** include a referral request, market update, or any business ask in a birthday message. This is the one message per year that is purely about the relationship.
- Keep the text variant to one sentence, two max
- Keep the email variant under 100 words
- If the client's `my_profile.md` or notes mention a personal detail (partner's name, kids, hobby), reference it — that's the difference between a memorable birthday message and a forgettable one
- Use emoji sparingly: one cake emoji max
- NEVER use "rest assured," "unprecedented," or any corporate language
