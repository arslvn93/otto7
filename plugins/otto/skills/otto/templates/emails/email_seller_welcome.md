# Seller Welcome Email

**Template Type:** Email — First contact after signing a listing agreement
**Tone:** Warm, confident, organized. This email sets the tone for the entire relationship. The seller should finish reading it thinking "I picked the right agent."
**When to use:** Within 24 hours of signing the listing agreement.

---

## Required Details

- Seller name(s)
- Property address
- Listing price
- Target list date (when the home goes live on MLS)
- Photography/staging date if scheduled
- Any immediate action items for the seller

---

## Email Template

**Subject line options (pick one):**
- We're officially live — here's what happens next
- Your listing game plan — [Property Address]
- Next steps for [Property Address] — let's get you sold

---

**Email body:**

Hi [Seller Name(s)],

Thank you for trusting me with the sale of [Property Address]. I take that seriously, and my goal is to make this process as smooth and profitable as possible for you.

I wanted to lay out exactly what happens from here so you always know what's coming next.

**Here's our timeline:**

**This week:**
- I'll schedule professional photography and, if applicable, a staging consultation. [If already scheduled: "We have photos booked for [date]."]
- I'll finalize the MLS listing and prepare all marketing materials.
- If there are any minor prep items to address before photos (decluttering, touch-up paint, landscaping cleanup), I'll send you a checklist.

**Launch day — [Target List Date]:**
- Your home goes live on the MLS, which feeds to Zillow, Realtor.com, Redfin, and hundreds of other sites.
- I'll share the listing across social media and my professional network.
- [If applicable: "We'll host our first open house on [date]."]

**Once we're live:**
- I'll send you a weekly update every [day of week] with showing activity, feedback, and any market changes.
- All showing requests come through me — I'll coordinate everything and make sure you have advance notice.
- When offers come in, I'll present each one with a clear breakdown of terms, not just price, so you can make an informed decision.

**A few things that help the process:**
- Keep the home showing-ready as much as possible (I know it's a hassle — I'll keep the disruption to a minimum).
- Let me know about any schedules, blackout dates, or situations I should be aware of.
- Questions at any point — text, call, or email. There are no dumb questions, and I'd rather you ask than wonder.

I'm genuinely excited about this listing. [One specific positive comment about the property — e.g., "That kitchen renovation is going to photograph beautifully" or "Homes on your street have been getting strong activity"]. Let's get you a great result.

Talk soon,

[Agent Name]
[Phone]
[Email]

---

## Tone Notes for Claude

- This email should feel like a confident project manager taking the wheel — organized, reassuring, and human.
- Don't be generic. Include at least one specific comment about the property or the seller's situation.
- The weekly update commitment is important — it builds trust and manages expectations. Don't skip it.
- Keep it under 400 words. Sellers are stressed and busy; respect their time.
- Never promise a specific outcome ("we'll definitely get multiple offers"). Promise a process and a commitment to communication.
