# Notice of Fulfilment — Email to Client

**Template Type:** Email — sent to client once all conditions have been waived, along with the NOF document
**Tone:** Warm, clear, professional. This is a "we did it" moment but also the moment the deal becomes legally binding.
**When to use:** The same day all conditions are waived.

---

## Required Details

- Client name(s)
- Property address
- Accepted price
- Closing date
- List of conditions being waived
- Attached NOF document

---

## Email Template

**Subject line options:**
- Conditions waived — [Property Address] is now FIRM
- Notice of Fulfilment for [Property Address] — please review and sign
- All clear — [Property Address] is firm (NOF attached)

---

**Email body:**

Hi [Client Name(s)],

Great news — we've completed all of the conditions on **[Property Address]**. Here's the summary:

- ✅ [Condition 1] — satisfied
- ✅ [Condition 2] — satisfied
- ✅ [Condition 3] — satisfied

Attached to this email is the **Notice of Fulfilment (NOF)** — the document that officially waives all the conditions and makes the deal **firm**.

**What I need from you:**

1. Review the NOF (it's short — one page)
2. Sign where indicated
3. Return it to me by **[deadline]** so I can deliver it to the listing agent and lock this in

Once I receive your signed NOF, I'll deliver it to the listing side and the deal officially goes firm. At that point:

- There are no more escape hatches on either side
- Your lawyer takes over the bulk of the closing paperwork
- We move into the runway to closing on **[Closing Date]**

I'll send a separate email shortly with the full path to closing — what your lawyer will do, what you need to do, and when. For now, just focus on getting this NOF back to me as quickly as possible.

Huge congratulations — this is the real milestone. [Property Address] is effectively yours.

[Agent Sign-Off]

[Signature Block]

---

## Tone Notes for Claude

- Warm and celebratory but not over-the-top — the firm-deal congratulations email will carry more of the celebration
- Clear instructions on what the client needs to do
- Under 300 words
- Always mention that the NOF is attached (even though Otto can't attach it — the agent will)
