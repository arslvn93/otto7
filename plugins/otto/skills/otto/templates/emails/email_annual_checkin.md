# Annual Check-In Email

**Template Type:** Email — Yearly touchpoint with past clients
**Tone:** Personal, brief, and valuable. This isn't a "just checking in" email — it provides a reason to reach out (home value update) and keeps the agent top of mind without being annoying.
**When to use:** Once a year, around the anniversary of their purchase or closing date. Can also be used around the new year or at a natural touchpoint (spring market kickoff, etc.).

---

## Required Details

- Client name(s)
- Property address
- Original purchase price (or what they sold for, if seller client)
- Approximate current value or market trend in their area
- Any personal detail the agent remembers (kids, job, home projects they mentioned)

---

## Email Template

**Subject line options:**
- Your home's value — one year later
- Quick update on [Neighborhood / Street Name]
- Checking in, [Client Name] — and your home's doing great
- Happy house-iversary!

---

Hi [Client Name(s)],

Can you believe it's been [one year / X years] since you [bought / sold] [Property Address]? Time flies.

I wanted to send a quick update because I know your home is more than just where you live — it's also one of the biggest investments you've made.

**Here's where your neighborhood stands:**

[2-3 sentences of market context. The agent should provide this data. Examples:]
- "Homes in [Neighborhood] have appreciated roughly [X]% over the past year. Based on recent comparable sales, your home's estimated value is in the range of $[range] — up from your purchase price of $[original price]."
- "The market in [Area] has been steady this year. Inventory is still low, which means your equity position remains strong. If you're curious about a more detailed valuation, I'm happy to run one for you."
- "It's been a shifting market, but [Neighborhood] has held up well. Your area continues to attract buyers because of [specific reason — schools, walkability, new development nearby]."

[Personal touch — one line that shows you remember them as a person, not just a transaction. Examples:]
- "How's the garden coming along? I remember you had big plans for that backyard."
- "I hope the kids are loving the new school — that was such a big factor in your search."
- "Did you end up finishing that basement renovation? I'd love to see how it turned out."

**If you ever need anything:**

- Thinking about refinancing? I can connect you with a great lender.
- Need a contractor, plumber, electrician, or handyman? I have a referral list of people I trust.
- Curious about selling? I can run a detailed market analysis — no obligation, just information.
- Know someone who's buying or selling? I'd love to help them the way I helped you.

Hope you're doing well. Don't be a stranger.

[Agent Name]
[Phone]
[Email]

## Tone Notes for Claude

- This email has one job: remind the client that their agent exists, cares, and is useful. That's it.
- The home value update is the hook — everyone wants to know what their home is worth. But the agent MUST provide the data. Never fabricate or estimate values.
- The personal touch line is what separates this from a CRM drip email. If the agent doesn't remember a personal detail, ask: "Is there anything personal you remember about this client I can reference?"
- The "if you ever need anything" section positions the agent as a resource, not a salesperson. The referral ask is one line buried naturally — not the point of the email.
- Keep it under 250 words. This is a touchpoint, not a newsletter.
- If the agent wants to send this to a large batch of past clients, remind them to personalize at least the name and property address. Mass emails that feel mass-produced do more harm than good.

---

## Variant — Seller Past Client (They No Longer Own the Property)

Adjust the hook since a home value update isn't relevant:

"Hi [Client Name], it's been [X years] since we sold [Property Address], and I just wanted to check in. How's everything going [in the new place / in [new city] / with the family]?

The market in [their old area] has been [brief note] — just in case you're curious about what the old neighborhood is doing.

I'm still here if you ever need anything real estate related — whether it's for you or someone you know. Hope you're doing great."
