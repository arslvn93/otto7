# Firm Deal Congrats + Path to Closing (Buyer)

**Template Type:** Email — sent the moment all conditions are waived and the deal goes firm
**Tone:** Celebratory and reassuring. Buyer has just signed away their escape hatch — they need confidence, not more warnings.
**When to use:** Within 1 hour of condition waiver signatures.

---

## Required Details

- Buyer name(s)
- Property address
- Firm date
- Closing date
- Buyer's lawyer name

---

## Email Template

**Subject line options:**
- It's firm — [Property Address] is officially yours
- Congratulations — the deal is done, now here's the path to closing
- [Property Address] is locked in — what happens between now and keys day

---

**Email body:**

Hi [Buyer Name(s)],

**[Property Address] is officially firm.** No more conditions, no more escape hatches on either side. You are legally buying this home on **[Closing Date]**. Congratulations — this is the real milestone.

Take a breath. Enjoy it. Then let me walk you through what the runway to closing looks like so you know exactly what happens between now and keys in hand.

**Your lawyer takes the lead from here**

[Lawyer Name]'s office is now driving the paperwork side of the transaction. You should expect:

- A phone call or email from them within the next few days to set up your file
- A request for ID and documentation (they'll tell you exactly what)
- Title search and closing document preparation
- A final statement of adjustments a few days before closing, showing exactly what you owe at closing

If you don't hear from them within a week, call them — don't wait.

**What YOU need to do between now and closing**

**Finalize financing**
- Confirm with your lender that your mortgage file is moving to fund
- They'll instruct your lawyer on when and how the funds transfer
- Expect a few more signatures — nothing dramatic, just loan paperwork

**Home insurance — before closing day**
- You need a binder for home insurance effective on closing day
- Your lender will require this before they release funds
- Start getting quotes now — you need this at least a week before closing

**Utilities**
- Set up new accounts (hydro/electric, gas, water, internet) effective closing day
- If you're moving out of a rental or existing home, schedule cancellation for the same day
- Don't forget: garbage/recycling, property tax registration (in some markets)

**Change of address**
- Start the list now: bank, employer, government (driver's license, health card), subscriptions, deliveries
- Canada Post / USPS mail forwarding — schedule it to start on closing day

**Movers**
- Book movers now if you haven't. Good movers are booked weeks out, especially at month-end.
- Plan to move the day after closing if possible — closing day itself can run late.

**The week of closing**

- Expect a **pre-closing walkthrough** 24–48 hours before closing day. I'll schedule this with the listing agent. You'll confirm the home is in the same condition, all agreed inclusions are there, and nothing has been damaged.
- Your lawyer will email you a final closing cost summary. Review it carefully.
- You'll likely need to wire the closing funds to your lawyer a day or two before closing. Never wire money based on email instructions alone — **always call your lawyer's office to verbally confirm the wire details before sending.**

**Closing day itself**

- Your lawyer handles the legal and financial side
- Funds transfer in the morning
- Keys are released once the deal registers — usually mid to late afternoon
- I'll be in touch as soon as I hear the keys are ready, and we'll meet at the property for the handoff

**After I hand you the keys**

- I'm not disappearing. Expect me to check in at the 2-week, 3-month, and 1-year marks.
- If anything comes up in the first year — contractor recommendations, questions about the home, neighborhood stuff — I'm your call.
- And when you know someone else thinking about buying or selling, please think of me. Referrals are how I build my business and I treat every one like family.

---

This is the fun part. You get to start imagining your life in this home — which room the couch goes in, what you'll do with the yard, where the Christmas tree will be. Go ahead and daydream.

Any questions between now and closing, you know where to find me.
