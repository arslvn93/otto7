# Offer Process Review Email (Buyer)

**Template Type:** Email — educational walkthrough of how the offer process actually works
**Tone:** Teacher mode. Confident, plain-language, demystifying. Buyers (especially first-timers) panic at offer stage because nobody explained it.
**When to use:** Once the buyer is actively looking and you can tell an offer is likely within the next few weeks.

---

## Required Details

- Buyer name(s)
- Whether they're a first-time buyer
- Market conditions (hot / balanced / slow) — affects the tone of strategy parts
- Typical timelines in the agent's market

---

## Email Template

**Subject line options:**
- How the offer process actually works (so nothing surprises you)
- Your game plan for when we're ready to make an offer
- Everything you need to know before we write an offer

---

**Email body:**

Hi [Buyer Name(s)],

Before we find the right home, I want to walk you through exactly how the offer process works so that when the moment comes, you're not learning it on the fly. Offer day is stressful enough — you shouldn't also be learning new vocabulary.

**Step 1 — We decide to write an offer**

When we find a home that checks your boxes, I'll pull the comps, walk you through what the home is realistically worth, and we'll decide together on a price. I'll tell you what I'd pay if it were me, and why. You make the final call.

**Step 2 — We draft the offer**

An offer has a few main parts:
- **Price** — what you're offering to pay
- **Deposit** — money you put down now to show you're serious. Typically [5% of offer price / $X-Y range for your market]. This money counts toward your down payment.
- **Closing date** — when you take possession
- **Conditions** — things that have to happen before the deal is firm (financing, home inspection, status certificate for condos, sale of your current home, etc.)
- **Inclusions/exclusions** — what stays with the home and what leaves

Each of these is a lever we can pull to make the offer stronger or more protective of you.

**Step 3 — We submit the offer**

I send it to the listing agent, and we wait for a response. The seller can accept, reject, or counter. Most of the time they counter.

**Step 4 — Negotiation**

If they counter, we have to decide: accept their terms, counter back, or walk. This part moves fast. We might go back and forth 2–3 times in one evening. Keep your phone on.

**Step 5 — Acceptance**

Once we agree on all terms and both sides sign, the offer is **accepted**. At this point you're "under contract" but the deal is still **conditional** on whatever conditions we wrote in.

**Step 6 — Working through conditions**

This is the period (usually [5-10 business days]) where we do the home inspection, finalize financing, review the status certificate if it's a condo, and anything else we built in. If everything checks out, we waive the conditions and the deal goes **firm**.

**Step 7 — Firm to closing**

Once firm, the deal is legally binding and moves toward the closing date. Your lawyer takes over most of the paperwork. I stay involved for the pre-closing walkthrough and key handoff.

---

**A few things to know about the emotional side**

- **Multiple offers happen.** In this market it's common. If that's the situation, I'll coach you through it — we'll decide in advance how high you'll go and stick to the plan.
- **You will feel like you're overpaying.** Almost every buyer feels this. It usually goes away within 90 days.
- **Not getting a home hurts.** It just does. If it happens, we debrief and go again. The right one is still out there.
- **Don't shop for furniture during the conditional period.** Seriously. Wait until the deal is firm.

**One thing I need from you**

When we find the home, you need to be reachable. Offer situations can move from "let's talk about it" to "decide in 30 minutes" in the space of a single phone call. Have your partner, your mortgage broker, and your calendar ready.

Any questions on any of this? Call or text any time — I'd rather answer the question now than in the middle of a bidding war.
