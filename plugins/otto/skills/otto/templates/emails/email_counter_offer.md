# Counter-Offer Email

**Template Type:** Email — Delivering a counter-offer from the seller to the buyer's agent
**Tone:** Professional, precise, and collegial. Agent-to-agent communication should be clear, respectful, and focused on getting the deal done.
**When to use:** When the seller wants to counter on price, terms, or both. This goes to the buyer's agent, not the buyer directly.

---

## Required Details

- Buyer's agent name
- Buyer name(s)
- Seller name(s)
- Property address
- Original offer price
- Counter-offer price
- Changes to terms (if any — closing date, contingencies, concessions, inclusions/exclusions, earnest money)
- Counter-offer expiration date/time
- Any context the listing agent wants to convey (seller's motivation, flexibility signals, or firm positions)

---

## Email Template

**Subject line options:**
- Counter-offer — [Property Address]
- [Property Address] — seller's counter attached
- Counter from seller — [Property Address]

---

Hi [Buyer's Agent Name],

Thank you for your client's offer on [Property Address]. My seller appreciates the interest and would like to counter as follows:

**Counter-Offer Terms:**

- **Price:** $[Counter Price] (original offer: $[Original Price])
[Include any other changed terms below. Only list what's changing — don't restate terms that remain the same.]
- **Closing Date:** [New date if changed, or "Per original offer"]
- **Seller Concessions:** [Changed amount or "None" or "Per original offer"]
- **Earnest Money:** [Changed amount if applicable]
- **Contingencies:** [Any changes — e.g., "Seller requests inspection contingency shortened to 7 days from 10"]
- **Personal Property:** [Any inclusions or exclusions — e.g., "Refrigerator and washer/dryer are excluded from the sale"]
- **Other:** [Any additional terms — rent-back, repair credits, home warranty, etc.]

**This counter-offer expires at [Time] on [Date].**

[Optional context paragraph — use when the agent wants to signal something without putting it in writing on the contract. Examples:]

- "My seller is motivated to close before [date] and is flexible on timeline if that helps your client's situation."
- "The price is firm based on the recent comp at [address] that closed at $[price], but my seller is open to discussing the concession request."
- "We have continued showing activity and my seller wanted me to communicate that this counter represents their best position at this time."
- [If no additional context is needed, skip this paragraph entirely.]

The formal counter-offer document is attached. Please let me know if your client has any questions or would like to discuss further.

Looking forward to working this out.

[Agent Name]
[Phone]
[Email]
[Brokerage]

---

## Tone Notes for Claude

- This is agent-to-agent communication. Keep it professional and efficient — no fluff, no emotional language.
- Only list the terms that are changing. Restating everything from the original offer creates confusion about what actually moved.
- The context paragraph is optional and strategic. It's where the listing agent can send signals that don't belong in the contract — flexibility, firmness, urgency, competing interest. If the agent provides this context, include it. If not, don't fabricate it.
- Always include the expiration clearly. Missing this is a rookie mistake.
- Never reveal the seller's bottom line, personal situation, or negotiation strategy unless the agent explicitly asks you to.
- If the agent describes a complex multi-point counter, organize it clearly. Confusion kills deals.

## Variant — Multiple Counter-Offers

When the seller is countering multiple buyers simultaneously:

**Additional language:**
"Please be advised that my seller is issuing counter-offers to multiple parties. This counter is non-binding until fully executed by all parties. I'd encourage your client to respond promptly."

**Note for Claude:** Multiple counter-offers are a specific legal process in many states. The agent should confirm with their broker that the proper forms and disclosures are being used. This email is supplementary to — not a replacement for — the formal counter-offer documents.

---

## Variant — Final Counter / Best and Final

When the seller is making their final counter or requesting best-and-final offers:

**Subject line:**
- Final counter — [Property Address]
- Best and final requested — [Property Address]

**Additional language:**
"My seller has asked me to communicate that this represents their final position on [price/terms/both]. [If requesting best and final from multiple buyers: 'We are requesting best and final offers from all interested parties by [time] on [date].']"
