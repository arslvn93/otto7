# New Listing Alert Email

**Template Type:** Email — Notifying a buyer about a property that matches their criteria
**Tone:** Excited but not pushy. The agent is bringing them something specific and relevant, not blasting a generic alert. This should feel personal.
**When to use:** When a listing hits the market (or comes to the agent's attention off-market) that closely matches what the buyer is looking for.

---

## Required Details

- Buyer name(s)
- Property address
- List price
- Key features that match the buyer's criteria
- What makes this one worth their attention (not just that it exists)
- Link to listing (MLS or portal)
- Showing availability / urgency context

---

## Email Template

**Subject line options:**
- This one checks your boxes — [Address or Neighborhood]
- Just hit the market: [Beds]/[Baths] in [Neighborhood] — $[Price]
- Worth a look? New listing in [Area]
- [Buyer Name], I think this might be it

[2-3 sentences connecting this property to what the buyer specifically told you they want. This is the most important part — it's what makes this feel personal, not automated.]

**What you should know:**

[1-2 sentences of honest context — anything the listing photos won't tell them.]

Examples:
- "The photos don't show it, but the backyard backs up to a creek with no rear neighbors. Much more private than it looks online."
- "Fair warning: the carpet and paint are dated, but those are easy fixes. The layout and location are hard to find at this price."
- "This area has been competitive lately — the last two comparable homes went under contract within a week."

**Next step:**

[Call to action based on urgency level.]

- Urgent: "This one isn't going to sit. If you're interested, let me know today and I'll get us in for a showing before the weekend."
- Off-market: "This isn't on the MLS yet — the listing agent gave me a heads up. We have a window to see it before it goes public. Let me know if you want in."

## Tone Notes for Claude

- This email lives or dies on specificity. Generic "check out this listing" emails get ignored. The "Why I'm flagging this one" section must reference the buyer's actual criteria.
- Keep it short. Buyers get a lot of listing alerts. This should take 30 seconds to read and immediately tell them why this one matters.
- Don't oversell the property. If it has trade-offs, mention them honestly — it builds trust and saves everyone a wasted showing.
- Match the urgency to the market. In a hot market, create real urgency ("this won't last"). In a slower market, keep it relaxed ("worth a look when you're free").
- If the agent doesn't provide the buyer's specific criteria, ask for it. A generic alert is worse than no alert.
