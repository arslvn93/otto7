# Open House Follow-Up Email

**Template Type:** Email — Follow-up to open house visitors
**Tone:** Warm, helpful, and not pushy. Most open house visitors are early in their search or are neighbors being nosy. Both are valuable — one is a potential buyer, the other is a future listing. Treat everyone like a relationship, not a lead to close.
**When to use:** Within 24 hours of the open house. Same day is ideal — while the home is still fresh in their mind.

---

## Required Details

- Visitor name (from sign-in sheet)
- Property address
- Open house date
- Any notes the agent remembers about the visitor (what they said, what they liked, whether they seemed serious)
- Whether the visitor is working with an agent
- Any updates on the listing (multiple interested parties, upcoming offer deadline, etc.)

---

## Template A — Interested Buyer (Showed Real Interest)

**Subject line options:**
- Great meeting you at [Property Address]
- Following up from Saturday's open house
- Thoughts on [Property Address]?

---

Hi [Visitor Name],

It was great meeting you at the open house at [Property Address] [today/yesterday/on Saturday]. Thanks for taking the time to come through.

[Personal reference — something they said or showed interest in. Examples:]
- "You mentioned loving the backyard — it really is one of the best lots in the neighborhood."
- "I could tell the kitchen caught your eye. The renovation was done last year and the sellers spared no expense."
- "You asked about the school district — [School Name] is one of the highest rated in the area, and it's less than a mile away."

If you'd like to take a closer look or have any questions about the property, I'm happy to arrange a private showing at a time that works for you.

[If there's urgency: "I want to be transparent — we've had strong interest and the sellers may be reviewing offers soon. If this home is on your radar, sooner is better than later."]

[If the visitor mentioned they're not working with an agent: "I'd also love to help with your broader search if you're still exploring the market. No pressure — just happy to be a resource."]

[If the visitor mentioned they have an agent: "Feel free to have your agent reach out to me directly if you'd like to schedule a second showing or discuss the property further."]

Let me know if there's anything I can help with.

## Tone Notes for Claude

- The personal reference in Template A is what makes the difference. If the agent provides notes from the sign-in sheet ("loved the kitchen," "asked about schools," "came with two kids"), use them. If not, use Template C.
- Never be pushy about urgency unless there's real urgency. Fake scarcity burns trust.
- The neighbor follow-up is an underrated lead generation tool. Every neighbor who visits an open house is thinking about what their home is worth. Plant the seed gently.
- Ask the agent: "Did anyone stand out as seriously interested? And did anyone mention whether they're working with another agent?" This changes the tone significantly.
- Keep all versions under 200 words. Open house visitors get follow-ups from every agent — yours needs to be the one they actually read.
