# Referral Request Email

**Template Type:** Email — Asking a past client for referrals
**Tone:** Grateful, low-pressure, and specific. This isn't a mass blast — it should feel like a personal note from someone who genuinely valued the relationship.
**When to use:** 2-3 weeks after closing. The client has settled in, the goodwill is high, and the experience is still fresh.

---

## Required Details

- Client name(s)
- Property address (the one you helped them with)
- Whether they were a buyer or seller
- Any personal detail to reference (how their move went, their kids settling into school, how they're enjoying the new kitchen, etc.)
- Where the agent wants referrals sent (phone, email, text)

---

## Email Template

**Subject line options:**
- Quick favor, [Client Name]?
- Hope you're settled in — and a small ask
- Loving the new place?

---

Hi [Client Name(s)],

I hope you're settling into [Property Address / "the new place"] and enjoying it. [Personal touch — e.g., "Has the backyard been put to use yet?" or "How are the kids liking the new school?" or "I bet that kitchen has already seen some action."]

I wanted to reach out with a small ask.

If you know anyone — a friend, coworker, family member, neighbor — who's thinking about buying or selling a home, I'd really appreciate it if you'd pass along my name. It doesn't have to be someone who's ready tomorrow — even someone who's just starting to think about it. I'm always happy to answer questions with no pressure.

The easiest way is to just share my contact info:

**[Agent Name]**
[Phone]
[Email]
[Website if applicable]

Or if it's easier, just text me their name and I'll reach out personally.

Real estate is a referral business, and working with people like you is exactly why I love what I do. Thank you again for trusting me — it meant a lot.

[Agent Name]

---

## Tone Notes for Claude

- This email should feel like it was written by a human, not a CRM. One personal reference makes all the difference.
- The ask should be specific ("a friend, coworker, family member") rather than vague ("anyone you know"). Specific prompts trigger specific names in the reader's mind.
- "It doesn't have to be someone who's ready tomorrow" lowers the bar and increases the likelihood they'll think of someone.
- Never make this transactional ("I pay referral bonuses!" or "Earn a gift card!"). The relationship is the incentive.
- Keep it under 200 words. This is a quick note, not a pitch.
- If the agent wants to include a review request alongside the referral ask, it can work — but keep them separate. Don't bury the referral ask inside a review request or vice versa.

---

## Variant — Combined Referral + Review Request

If the agent wants to ask for a Google/Zillow review in the same email, add this after the referral ask:

"One more thing — if you had a good experience working with me, a quick review on [Google / Zillow / Realtor.com] would mean a lot. It helps other buyers and sellers find me, and it only takes a minute.

Here's the direct link: [Review Link]

No pressure at all — and thank you either way."
