# CMA Cover Letter

**Template Type:** Letter — Accompanies a Comparative Market Analysis presentation
**Tone:** Professional, data-informed, and confident without being arrogant. This positions the agent as a knowledgeable advisor, not a salesperson.
**When to use:** Included at the front of a CMA package before a listing appointment, or emailed alongside a digital CMA.

---

## Required Details

- Seller / homeowner name(s)
- Property address
- Agent's recommended price range (or specific price)
- Number of comparable sales analyzed
- Key market context (average DOM in the area, inventory levels, price trends)
- One specific strength of the property
- One honest note about the competitive landscape
- Date of the CMA

Dear [Homeowner Name(s)],

Thank you for the opportunity to prepare this market analysis for [Property Address]. Whether you're actively planning to sell or simply want to understand where you stand, I want to make sure you have clear, honest data to work with.

**What's inside this report:**

I've analyzed [number] comparable properties — homes that have recently sold, are currently listed, or were listed and didn't sell — in your area. These are the same properties that buyers and their agents will use to evaluate your home, and the same data an appraiser will reference if the buyer is financing.

**What the market is telling us:**

[2-3 sentences of market context. Example: "In [neighborhood/area], the average home is selling in [X] days at [X]% of list price. Inventory is [low/moderate/high], which means [what that implies for the seller — more competition from buyers, more leverage in negotiations, etc.]. Over the past [3/6/12] months, prices have [trended up/remained stable/softened slightly]."]

**Your home's position:**

[1-2 sentences about the property's specific strengths relative to comps. Example: "Your renovated kitchen and the lot size put you ahead of most comparable properties in the neighborhood. The one area where we'll face competition is [honest note — e.g., newer construction on the north side of the subdivision, or similar homes priced aggressively]."]

Based on this analysis, I recommend a listing price in the range of **$[Low End] – $[High End]**. I'll walk you through how I got there and what each pricing strategy means for your timeline and bottom line.

**What I'd like to discuss when we meet:**

- The pricing strategy that aligns with your goals and timeline
- My marketing plan for your home — how we get maximum exposure to the right buyers
- The current offer landscape — what kinds of offers sellers in your area are receiving and how to evaluate them
- Your questions — anything about the process, the market, or the numbers in this report

I'm looking forward to our conversation. I don't take lightly that selling your home is one of the biggest financial decisions you'll make, and I want you to feel completely informed and confident in whatever you decide.

Sincerely,

[Agent Name]
[Phone]
[Email]
[Brokerage]

---

## Tone Notes for Claude

- This letter should feel like it was written by a sharp, trustworthy advisor — not a form letter.
- The "honest note" about competitive landscape is critical. It builds credibility. Sellers can smell an agent who only tells them what they want to hear.
- Never include a guaranteed outcome or timeline promise.
- The recommended price should be a range unless the agent specifically asks for a single number.
- Keep it to one page. This is a cover letter, not the analysis itself.
- If the agent doesn't provide market context data, ask for it. Do not fabricate statistics.
