# Listing Description Generator

**Template Type:** MLS Property Description
**Tone:** Confident, descriptive, specific. Paint a picture without overselling. Let the home's features speak.
**Compliance:** Fair Housing compliant. No references to protected classes. No promissory language.

---

## Before You Write — Ask For These Details

- Property address
- Beds / Baths / Square footage
- Year built
- Lot size
- Property type (single family, condo, townhome, multi-family)
- Key features (open floor plan, vaulted ceilings, hardwood floors, fireplace, etc.)
- Recent upgrades with dates (new roof 2023, renovated kitchen 2024, etc.)
- Outdoor features (pool, deck, patio, fenced yard, garage details)
- Neighborhood highlights (walkability, schools, parks, dining, commute info)
- HOA details if applicable (monthly fee, what it covers)
- Target buyer profile (first-time buyer, growing family, downsizer, investor)
- Asking price
- Any unique selling points (views, lot position, historical significance, smart home features)
- Character limit if needed (many MLS systems cap at 1,000 characters for public remarks)

---

## Structure — How to Build the Description

**Opening line:** Lead with the single most compelling thing about this property. Not "Welcome to..." — something specific that hooks attention.

**Paragraph 1 — First impression and flow:** What you experience walking in. Layout, light, architectural details, flooring, ceiling height.

**Paragraph 2 — Kitchen and living spaces:** The rooms where people spend the most time. Appliances, countertops, cabinetry, open-to-living flow, entertaining potential.

**Paragraph 3 — Bedrooms and bathrooms:** Primary suite first (size, closet, en-suite details), then secondary bedrooms. Bathroom finishes.

**Paragraph 4 — Outdoor and bonus spaces:** Yard, patio, deck, pool, garage, basement, bonus room, home office, storage.

**Closing line:** Location context and call to action. What's nearby, why this neighborhood, and a reason to schedule a showing.

## Variant A — Standard Residential

```
[COMPELLING OPENING ABOUT THE PROPERTY'S STANDOUT FEATURE]

[FIRST IMPRESSION — layout, light, architectural details, flooring]

[KITCHEN AND LIVING — appliances, countertops, flow, entertaining]

[BEDROOMS AND BATHS — primary suite, secondary rooms, finishes]

[OUTDOOR AND BONUS — yard, patio, garage, extra spaces]

[LOCATION AND CTA — neighborhood, proximity, reason to see it in person]
```

**Example opening lines (adapt, don't copy):**
- "Fully renovated 4-bed colonial on a quiet cul-de-sac with a brand-new chef's kitchen and a backyard built for entertaining."
- "2,400 square feet of single-level living with 10-foot ceilings, an open floor plan, and a half-acre lot backing to protected woods."
- "Mid-century modern meets 2024 upgrades — original character plus a new roof, new HVAC, and a completely reimagined primary suite."

## Variant B — Luxury Property

**Tone shift:** More editorial. Longer sentences. Emphasize lifestyle and experience, not just specs.

```
[LIFESTYLE-ORIENTED OPENING — what it feels like to live here]

[ARCHITECTURAL NARRATIVE — design details, materials, craftsmanship]

[KITCHEN AS CENTERPIECE — brand-name appliances, custom details, entertaining flow]

[PRIMARY SUITE — retreat language, spa-like bath, closet details, views]

[OUTDOOR LIVING — pool, landscaping, outdoor kitchen, privacy, lot position]

[LOCATION AND EXCLUSIVITY — gated community, proximity to luxury amenities, privacy]
```

**Example opening lines:**
- "Set on over an acre behind a gated entry, this custom-built estate offers the kind of privacy and finish quality that rarely comes to market."
- "Floor-to-ceiling glass frames panoramic water views from nearly every room in this architecturally significant waterfront residence."

## Variant C — Starter Home / First-Time Buyer

**Tone shift:** Approachable and encouraging. Focus on value, move-in readiness, and "room to grow."

```
[VALUE-FORWARD OPENING — what makes this a smart buy]

[MOVE-IN READY DETAILS — recent updates, condition, no immediate work needed]

[PRACTICAL FEATURES — storage, parking, laundry, layout efficiency]

[OUTDOOR SPACE — even a small yard or patio matters at this price point]

[NEIGHBORHOOD AND AFFORDABILITY — commute, schools, shopping, price context vs. area]
```

**Example opening lines:**
- "Move-in ready 3-bedroom ranch with a new roof, updated kitchen, and a fenced backyard — all under $300K."
- "Why rent when you can own? This freshly updated 2-bed townhome is priced below most one-bedrooms in the area."

## Variant D — Investment Property

**Tone shift:** Numbers-focused. Speak to ROI, rental potential, and cash flow.

```
[INVESTMENT THESIS — why this property makes financial sense]

[CURRENT INCOME IF APPLICABLE — rent roll, occupancy, lease terms]

[PROPERTY CONDITION — recent capex, remaining useful life of major systems]

[UNIT MIX AND LAYOUT — if multi-family, describe each unit]

[LOCATION FOR RENTABILITY — employment centers, transit, universities, demand drivers]

[FINANCIALS AND CTA — cap rate, price per unit, cash-on-cash if appropriate]
```

**Example opening lines:**
- "Fully occupied duplex generating $3,200/month with long-term tenants and a new roof — 7.2% cap rate at asking."
- "Three-unit mixed-use building in the heart of downtown with street-level retail and two residential units above. Stable income with upside potential."

---

## Variant E — Condo / Townhome

**Tone shift:** Emphasize lifestyle, low maintenance, amenities, and community.

```
[LIFESTYLE OPENING — lock-and-leave, urban access, amenity-rich living]

[INTERIOR — layout, finishes, natural light, storage, in-unit laundry]

[BUILDING AMENITIES — gym, pool, rooftop, concierge, parking type]

[HOA DETAILS — monthly fee, what's included (water, insurance, reserves, etc.)]

[LOCATION — walkability, transit, dining, entertainment, commute]
```

**Example opening lines:**
- "Top-floor corner unit with floor-to-ceiling windows, in-unit laundry, and a private balcony — in a full-amenity building steps from downtown."
- "Lock the door and go. This beautifully updated 2-bed condo includes a dedicated parking spot, storage unit, and access to the building's pool and fitness center."

## Words and Phrases to AVOID

| Don't Say | Say Instead |
|-----------|-------------|
| Nestled | Describe the actual position: "set back from the road," "at the end of a cul-de-sac" |
| Boasts | Just state the feature directly |
| Stunning | Describe what makes it impressive: "10-foot coffered ceilings," "unobstructed mountain views" |
| Won't last / Don't miss out | "Showings begin Saturday" or "Open house this weekend" — create urgency with facts |
| Dream home | Describe the features and let the buyer decide |
| Turnkey | List the specific updates |
| Priced to sell | "Priced at/below recent comps in [neighborhood]" |
| A must-see | Describe what they'll see |
| Cozy (for small spaces) | "Efficient layout," "well-designed use of space" |
| Charming (for dated) | Describe the actual character features |
| Motivated seller | Avoid unless the agent specifically requests it |
| Walking distance | Give the actual distance or walk time |

---

## Formatting Rules

- Lead with the hook, not "Welcome to [address]"
- Sentence case — never ALL CAPS
- Avoid exclamation marks (one max in the entire description)
- Use em dashes for emphasis, not double hyphens
- Spell out numbers under 10 unless they're measurements (3 bed, 2 bath is fine)
- Keep under 1,000 characters for standard MLS unless told otherwise
- End with a reason to schedule a showing, not just "call today!"
