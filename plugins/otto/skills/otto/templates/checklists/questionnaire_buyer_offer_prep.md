# Buyer Offer Prep Questionnaire

**Template Type:** Short intake sent RIGHT before writing an offer on a specific home
**Tone:** Tactical, focused, no fluff. This is the "before we pull the trigger" checklist.
**When to use:** The same day the buyer says "I want to write an offer on this one."

---

## Required Details

- Buyer name(s)
- Subject property address
- Listed price
- Whether we already know there are competing offers

---

## Questionnaire Template

**Subject line:** Quick offer prep — [Property Address]

---

**Email intro:**

Hi [Buyer Name(s)],

Before we write the offer on [Property Address], I need to lock in a few things fast. Please answer the questions below — reply to this email or text me — and once I have these, I can draft the offer within the hour.

---

**1. Price strategy**
- What's the most you would pay for this home and walk away happy?
- What's your absolute ceiling — the number where, if they counter higher, you say no?
- How are you feeling about the listed price — low, fair, high?

**2. Deposit**
- Can you deliver [deposit amount] in certified funds / wire within 24 hours of acceptance?
- If deposit size matters in this deal, is there flexibility to go higher?

**3. Closing date**
- Ideal closing date
- Earliest you could close if the seller pushed
- Latest you'd accept if the seller pushed

**4. Conditions — what do you want built in?**
- Financing condition? (Yes = you need lender sign-off after acceptance; No = riskier but stronger offer)
- Home inspection condition? (Strongly recommend yes unless we did a pre-inspection)
- Status certificate review? (Condo only)
- Sale of your current home? (Only if applicable)
- Any other condition you want me to include?

**5. Inclusions / exclusions**
- Anything in the home you specifically want written into the offer (appliances, fixtures, etc.)?
- Anything you'd let the seller keep if they asked?

**6. Multiple offer contingency**
- If we find out there are competing offers, are you willing to go higher than the number in question 1?
- How much higher, and is there a hard stop?
- Are you comfortable dropping conditions if needed? Which ones?

**7. Your gut check**
- On a scale of 1–10, how badly do you want this specific home?
- If someone else buys it, will you be heartbroken or relieved?
- Is there anything about this home that's been nagging you — something you haven't quite said out loud?

---

**Close:**

Send these back as soon as you can. The clock matters — every hour we wait is an hour someone else could write an offer. Once I have your answers, I'll draft the offer and walk you through every line before we submit.

[Agent Sign-Off]

[Signature Block]

---

## Tone Notes for Claude

- This is the moment buyers freeze up. Keep the questions crisp and the stakes clear without being alarmist.
- Question 7 is the most important — the gut check reveals things the rational answers don't
- If the agent specifies it's a bidding war, rewrite the intro to be more urgent
- Keep under 500 words
