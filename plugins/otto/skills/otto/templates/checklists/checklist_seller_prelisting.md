# Seller Pre-Listing Checklist

**Template Type:** Checklist — Sent to sellers before photography and listing launch
**Tone:** Helpful and encouraging, not overwhelming. Frame everything as "quick wins that make a big difference in photos and first impressions."
**When to use:** As soon as the listing agreement is signed, ideally 1-2 weeks before the photography date.

---

## Required Details

- Seller name(s)
- Property address
- Photography date
- Any specific items the agent noticed during the listing appointment

---

## Checklist Template

**Subject line options:**
- Getting [Property Address] photo-ready — your checklist
- Quick prep list before photos on [Date]
- Easy wins to make [Property Address] shine

---

Hi [Seller Name(s)],

Photos are booked for [Date], and I want to make sure your home looks its absolute best. Buyers form their opinion in the first 5 seconds of scrolling — great photos are the single biggest factor in getting people through the door.

Here's a simple checklist. You don't need to do everything, but the more you can tackle, the better your photos (and your first impression) will be.

---

### Exterior — First Impression

- [ ] Mow the lawn, edge the walkways, and pull any visible weeds
- [ ] Clear the front porch/entry of clutter, old mats, and dead plants
- [ ] Power wash the driveway, walkways, and front porch if needed
- [ ] Make sure the house numbers are visible and clean
- [ ] Clean the front door — consider a fresh coat of paint if it's scuffed
- [ ] Put away garden hoses, tools, trash cans, and toys
- [ ] Add a fresh doormat and a potted plant by the front door (simple goes a long way)
- [ ] Clean exterior windows

### Kitchen — The Money Room

- [ ] Clear all countertops — remove small appliances, knife blocks, paper towel holders, dish soap
- [ ] Leave out 1-2 styled items max (a cutting board with fruit, a cookbook, a small plant)
- [ ] Clean the stovetop, oven front, and dishwasher front
- [ ] Remove everything from the front of the refrigerator (magnets, photos, notes)
- [ ] Organize inside the pantry and under the sink — buyers open everything
- [ ] Empty the trash can or hide it in the garage for photos

### Living Areas

- [ ] Remove personal photos from walls and shelves (buyers should picture their own family here)
- [ ] Reduce furniture if rooms feel crowded — less is more
- [ ] Remove or minimize kids' toys, pet items, and exercise equipment
- [ ] Fluff and arrange throw pillows, fold blankets neatly
- [ ] Open all blinds and curtains — let in maximum natural light
- [ ] Replace any burned-out light bulbs (every single one)
- [ ] Dust ceiling fans, light fixtures, and baseboards
- [ ] Remove area rugs that are worn or clashing — bare floors often photograph better

### Bedrooms

- [ ] Make all beds with clean, neutral-colored bedding if possible
- [ ] Clear nightstands down to one or two items
- [ ] Clean out and organize closets — half-full looks spacious, stuffed looks small
- [ ] Remove anything stored under beds
- [ ] Take down any overly personal or polarizing wall art

### Bathrooms

- [ ] Put out fresh, matching white towels (rolled or neatly folded)
- [ ] Remove all personal toiletries from counters and showers
- [ ] Clean mirrors, glass, fixtures, and grout
- [ ] Put the toilet lid down
- [ ] Add a small plant or candle for staging
- [ ] Empty the trash can or hide it

### Garage and Storage

- [ ] Organize the garage — neat and functional beats empty
- [ ] Clear enough space that buyers can see the floor and imagine parking
- [ ] Organize visible storage areas (basement, attic access, utility rooms)

### General — Whole Home

- [ ] Deep clean everything — floors, baseboards, windows, light switches
- [ ] Eliminate odors: no heavy air fresheners, but deal with pet smells, smoke, cooking odors
- [ ] Set the thermostat to a comfortable temperature for showings
- [ ] Secure or remove valuables, medications, and sensitive documents
- [ ] If you have pets, plan where they'll go during showings and open houses

---

### Don't Worry About

- Major repairs (unless we discussed something specific — those get handled through negotiation)
- Repainting entire rooms (unless the color is very bold or dark)
- Replacing flooring, fixtures, or appliances (buyers expect to pay for the home's current condition)

The goal isn't perfection — it's making every room feel clean, bright, and spacious. If you want to prioritize, focus on the kitchen, primary bedroom, and front entry. Those drive the most buyer reactions.

## Tone Notes for Claude

- This should feel helpful, not bossy. Sellers are already stressed about having strangers in their home.
- Include the "Don't Worry About" section — it prevents sellers from spiraling into expensive projects.
- If the agent mentions specific issues they noticed (e.g., "the front yard needs work" or "the master bath is dated"), weight the checklist toward those areas.
- Keep the energy positive. The subtext is: "your home is great, and these small things will make it photograph even better."
