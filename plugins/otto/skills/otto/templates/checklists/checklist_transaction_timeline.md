# Transaction Timeline Checklist

**Template Type:** Checklist — Full offer-to-close timeline with responsibilities and deadlines
**Tone:** Clear, organized, and reassuring. This is the client's roadmap through the most complex part of the process. It should eliminate surprises.
**When to use:** Send to the buyer (or seller) immediately after an offer is accepted. Customize the dates based on the contract terms.

---

## Required Details

- Client name(s) and whether they're the buyer or seller
- Property address
- Contract acceptance date
- Closing date
- Inspection contingency deadline
- Financing contingency deadline (if applicable)
- Appraisal contingency deadline (if applicable)
- Any other contractual deadlines (HOA doc review, sale contingency, etc.)

### Week 1 — Immediately After Contract Acceptance

**Days 1-3:**
- [ ] Deliver earnest money deposit to escrow/title company — **Deadline: [Date]**
- [ ] Agent sends executed contract to title company and lender
- [ ] Lender begins processing the loan — provide any additional documents they request immediately
- [ ] Agent orders title search

**Days 3-7:**
- [ ] Schedule home inspection — **Must be completed by: [Inspection Deadline Date]**
- [ ] Schedule any specialty inspections if needed (radon, termite/WDI, sewer scope, well/septic, mold)
- [ ] Review HOA documents if applicable — **Review deadline: [Date if applicable]**

---

### Week 2 — Inspection and Negotiation

- [ ] Attend home inspection (strongly recommended — usually takes 2-3 hours)
- [ ] Review inspection report with your agent
- [ ] Decide on repair requests, credits, or acceptance as-is
- [ ] Agent submits repair request to seller's agent (if applicable) — **Before inspection deadline: [Date]**
- [ ] Negotiate and finalize inspection resolution
- [ ] Remove inspection contingency (or exercise your right to cancel if issues are dealbreakers)

**Your action items this week:**
- Respond promptly to any lender requests for documents
- Get homeowner's insurance quotes — your lender will require a policy before closing. [Agent can recommend providers if needed.]

---

### Weeks 2-3 — Appraisal

- [ ] Lender orders appraisal (you don't schedule this — the lender handles it)
- [ ] Appraiser visits the property
- [ ] Appraisal report returned — **Typical turnaround: 5-10 business days**
- [ ] Review appraisal results:
  - If at or above purchase price: proceed as planned
  - If below purchase price: agent negotiates with seller (options include seller reducing price, buyer covering the gap, meeting in the middle, or canceling per appraisal contingency)
- [ ] Appraisal contingency deadline: **[Date]**

---

### Weeks 3-4 — Loan Approval and Closing Prep

- [ ] Lender issues conditional approval (you may need to provide additional documentation)
- [ ] Clear any remaining lender conditions promptly
- [ ] Receive Closing Disclosure (CD) — **Must be received at least 3 business days before closing**
- [ ] Review the CD carefully — check loan terms, monthly payment, closing costs, and credits
- [ ] Confirm closing date, time, and location with your agent
- [ ] Arrange wire transfer or cashier's check for closing funds (your title company will provide exact figures and wiring instructions)
- [ ] **Never wire money based on emailed instructions alone.** Always call your title company directly to verify wiring details. Wire fraud is real and common.
- [ ] Schedule final walk-through — **24-48 hours before closing**

---

### Closing Week

**Final walk-through:**
- [ ] Verify the home is in the same condition as when you went under contract
- [ ] Confirm any agreed-upon repairs were completed
- [ ] Check that all fixtures and included items are present
- [ ] Test lights, faucets, appliances, garage door, HVAC
- [ ] Report any issues to your agent immediately

**Closing day:**
- [ ] Bring valid government-issued photo ID (both buyers if applicable)
- [ ] Bring cashier's check or confirm wire has been sent (per title company instructions)
- [ ] Sign closing documents (plan for 45-60 minutes)
- [ ] Receive keys!

---

### After Closing

- [ ] Transfer utilities into your name
- [ ] Update your address (USPS, bank, employer, insurance, subscriptions)
- [ ] Rekey or change locks
- [ ] File for homestead exemption if applicable
- [ ] Save all closing documents for tax purposes
- [ ] Leave your agent a review (if you had a great experience)

## Seller Transaction Timeline

For sellers, the timeline is simpler but still important:

### After Accepting an Offer

- [ ] Agent sends executed contract to title company
- [ ] Provide title company with your mortgage payoff information
- [ ] Be available and responsive during the inspection period — buyer may request access for inspectors
- [ ] Review and respond to any inspection repair requests with your agent
- [ ] Keep the home in showing condition and maintained until closing
- [ ] Continue making mortgage payments until closing
- [ ] Do NOT remove any fixtures or included items from the property

### Before Closing

- [ ] Complete any agreed-upon repairs and keep receipts
- [ ] Schedule move-out so the home is empty and clean by closing (or by possession date if a rent-back is agreed)
- [ ] Cancel or transfer utilities effective on closing date
- [ ] Gather all keys, garage remotes, gate codes, mailbox keys, appliance manuals, and warranty documents
- [ ] Final clean of the property (broom-clean is the minimum; professional cleaning is better)

### Closing Day

- [ ] Bring valid government-issued photo ID
- [ ] Sign closing documents
- [ ] Deliver keys, remotes, and access devices to the buyer (usually through the agent or title company)
- [ ] Net proceeds are wired to your account (typically 1-2 business days)

### After Closing

- [ ] Set up mail forwarding through USPS
- [ ] Cancel homeowner's insurance, lawn care, pest control, security monitoring, and any other property-tied services
- [ ] Keep closing documents for tax purposes
- [ ] Forward any mail that arrives at the old address to the new owners

## Tone Notes for Claude

- This checklist should feel like a GPS for the transaction — clear, sequential, and confidence-building.
- Always customize the dates. A generic checklist without real deadlines is useless.
- The wire fraud warning is critical and should be included every time. It's a real and growing problem.
- For first-time buyers, add brief explanations of what each step means. For experienced buyers, keep it lean.
- If the agent doesn't provide the specific contingency deadlines, ask for them. This checklist doesn't work without real dates.
- The seller version is intentionally shorter — sellers have fewer action items but still need to know the timeline.
