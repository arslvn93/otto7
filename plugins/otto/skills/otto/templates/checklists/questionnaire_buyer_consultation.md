# Buyer Consultation Questionnaire

**Template Type:** Intake form — Used during or before the first buyer consultation
**Tone:** Conversational but thorough. This isn't a government form — it's a structured conversation that helps the agent understand what the buyer actually needs.
**When to use:** Send before the first meeting so the buyer can think through their answers, or use it as a guided conversation framework during the consultation.

---

## Required Details

- Buyer name(s)
- Date of consultation

---

## Tone Notes for Claude

- If the agent wants to send this as a form, format it cleanly with clear sections and space for answers.
- If the agent wants to use it as a conversation guide, reformat as a list of questions they can ask naturally.
- Not every buyer needs every question. For experienced buyers, skip the basic process questions. For investors, weight toward financials and condition tolerance. For first-timers, spend more time on the financing and process sections.
- The "what's your typical weekend" question is a sleeper — it reveals more about neighborhood fit than any checkbox.
- The internal notes section is for the agent only. Never include it in anything sent to the buyer.
