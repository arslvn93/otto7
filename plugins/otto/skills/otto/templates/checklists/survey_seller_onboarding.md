# Seller Onboarding Survey

**Template Type:** Intake survey — sent to seller right after listing agreement is signed
**Tone:** Friendly, thorough, not overwhelming. This replaces a 30-minute phone call.
**When to use:** Day 1 or 2 after signing, alongside or shortly after the welcome email.

---

## Required Details

- Seller name(s)
- Property address
- Target list date

---

## Survey Template

**Subject line:** A few quick questions before we go live — [Property Address]

---

**Email intro:**

Hi [Seller Name(s)],

Before we get [Property Address] to market, I want to make sure I have everything I need to represent you well and avoid surprises. Please take 5–10 minutes to answer the questions below — you can reply to this email or text me the answers, whichever is easier.

---

**1. Property history**
- When did you buy the home, and roughly what did you pay?
- Any major renovations, repairs, or upgrades (year and rough cost)?
- Any work done without permits?
- Is there a survey? Do you have it?

**2. Systems & mechanicals**
- How old is the roof?
- How old is the furnace / AC / water heater / HVAC?
- Any known issues — roof leaks, basement moisture, electrical, plumbing, foundation?
- Any appliances included in the sale? Any excluded?

**3. The neighborhood story**
- What do you love about living here?
- Favorite restaurants, coffee spots, schools, parks you want us to mention?
- Best commute or transit access?
- Anything a buyer should know that isn't obvious from the listing?

**4. Showings & logistics**
- Best days/times for showings?
- Any blackout dates (vacation, family events)?
- Pets we need to work around?
- Anyone living in the home besides the owner(s) (tenants, family)?
- Alarm code / gate access instructions?
- Any items in the home that are NOT staying (light fixtures, mirrors, wine fridge, etc.)?

**5. Your situation**
- Where are you going next? Any timing pressure we should know about?
- What's your ideal closing date?
- If we receive multiple offers, what matters most to you — price, closing date, conditions, something else?
- How do you prefer to be reached — text, call, or email?

**6. Financial / legal**
- Who is your real estate lawyer? (If you don't have one, I can recommend a few.)
- Any liens, HELOC, or existing mortgage details I should know about?
- Any divorce, estate, or POA situations that affect the sale?

---

**Close:**

Thanks for taking a few minutes on this. The more I know up front, the fewer surprises we both deal with later. Send the answers back whenever works — text, email, or a voice memo is fine.

Talk soon,

[Agent Name]
[Phone] · [Email]

---

## Tone Notes for Claude

- Frame every question as helping the seller, not as homework
- If the agent gave specifics about the seller's situation (estate sale, divorce, investment property), adapt questions accordingly and DROP any that don't apply
- Keep it scannable — don't turn it into a form document with dozens of sub-bullets
- Never ask for protected-class info (family size, kids' ages, religion, etc.)
