# Phone & Door-Knocking Prospecting Scripts

**Template Type:** Reference document — Verbal scripts for outbound prospecting
**Tone:** Confident, conversational, and respectful. These scripts are frameworks, not word-for-word recitations. The best prospecting feels like a natural conversation, not a telemarketing call.
**When to use:** Daily prospecting sessions. The agent should adapt these to their natural speaking voice.

---

## General Prospecting Rules

1. **Lead with value, not a pitch.** Give them something useful before asking for anything.
2. **Get to the point quickly.** Respect their time. You have 10-15 seconds before they decide whether to keep listening.
3. **Ask questions, don't lecture.** A conversation beats a monologue every time.
4. **Handle "no" gracefully.** "No" today often becomes "yes" in 6 months. Don't burn the bridge.
5. **Always have a next step.** Every conversation should end with either a meeting, a follow-up date, or permission to stay in touch.
6. **Comply with Do Not Call regulations.** Check the National Do Not Call Registry and your state's regulations before cold calling. FSBO and expired listings have different rules — check your state.

## Script 1 — Circle Prospecting (Neighbors of a New Listing or Recent Sale)

**Context:** Calling or knocking on doors near a home you just listed or just sold. The goal is to introduce yourself and plant a seed.

---

**Phone version:**

"Hi, is this [Name]? This is [Agent Name] with [Brokerage]. I'm calling because I just [listed / sold] a home on your street at [Address] — [brief detail: 'it sold for $X in X days' or 'it just hit the market at $X'].

The reason I'm reaching out is that whenever a home sells in a neighborhood, other homeowners are usually curious about what that means for their property value. Would you be interested in knowing what your home is worth in today's market?

[If yes:] Great — I can put together a quick market analysis and email it to you. What's the best email address?

[If no:] Totally understand. If you ever get curious, or if you know anyone thinking about buying or selling, I'd love to help. Can I leave you my number just in case?"

---

**Door-knock version:**

"Hi there — I'm [Agent Name] with [Brokerage]. I just wanted to stop by because I [listed / sold] the home at [Address] just down the street. [If sold: 'It sold for $X — that's great news for the neighborhood.']

I'm not here to pitch you anything — I just thought you might be curious about what that sale means for your home's value. I'm happy to run a quick market analysis for you if you'd like.

[Hand them a card or flyer.]

Either way, if you or anyone you know ever has questions about the market, I'm local and always happy to help."

## Script 2 — Expired Listing

**Context:** Calling a homeowner whose listing just expired (came off market without selling). They're likely frustrated with their previous agent. Be empathetic, not salesy.

---

"Hi [Name], this is [Agent Name] with [Brokerage]. I noticed your home at [Address] recently came off the market, and I wanted to reach out — not to give you a hard sell, but because I think I might be able to help if you're still interested in selling.

I know that's probably a frustrating experience, and I'm sure you're getting calls from every agent in town right now. So I'll keep this quick:

I took a look at your listing and I have a few thoughts on why it may not have sold and what I'd do differently. Would you be open to a quick conversation — maybe 15 minutes — to see if it's worth taking another shot?

[If yes:] Great. Would [day/time] work for a quick meeting? I'll bring some data and a fresh perspective.

[If no / not right now:] I completely understand. If anything changes, or if you just want a second opinion down the road, I'd be happy to help. Can I send you my info just in case?

[If they ask what you'd do differently:] A few things stood out to me: [mention 1-2 specific observations — pricing relative to comps, photo quality, marketing exposure, or DOM issues]. But I'd want to see the home in person before making any real recommendations. That's why I'm suggesting a quick meeting — no commitment, just a fresh set of eyes."

## Script 3 — For Sale By Owner (FSBO)

**Context:** Calling or visiting a homeowner who's trying to sell without an agent. Respect their decision. Offer value, not a guilt trip.

---

**Phone version:**

"Hi [Name], this is [Agent Name] with [Brokerage]. I saw that you're selling your home at [Address] on your own — and I respect that, it's not easy to take that on.

I'm not calling to convince you to hire me. I actually just wanted to offer a couple of things that might help regardless of whether you use an agent:

First, would you like a free market analysis so you can see how your price compares to recent sales in the area? It's the same data an appraiser would use, and it takes me about 10 minutes to pull together.

Second, if you're getting showings but not offers, I'm happy to share some feedback on what buyers in this market are looking for. No strings attached.

[If interested:] Great — what's the best email for me to send that over? And if you'd be open to it, I'd love to stop by and see the home. Sometimes a fresh perspective from someone who sees 20 homes a week can make a difference.

[If not interested:] Totally fair. I'll leave you my info just in case anything changes or you have questions down the road. Best of luck with the sale."

---

**Door-knock version:**

"Hi, I'm [Agent Name] with [Brokerage]. I saw your For Sale sign and wanted to stop by and introduce myself. I'm not here to talk you into hiring an agent — I just work the area and thought I could be a resource.

If you'd like, I can send you a market analysis showing recent sales on this street so you can see where your price falls. No cost, no obligation.

[Hand them a card.]

And if you ever want a second opinion on anything — pricing, marketing, negotiating an offer — I'm right down the road. Good luck with the sale."

## Script 4 — Past Client Check-In (Phone)

**Context:** Calling a past client you haven't spoken to in a while. The goal is to stay top of mind and generate referrals.

---

"Hey [Name], it's [Agent Name]! How are you? How's everything at [their address / 'the house']?

[Let them talk. Ask about something personal you remember.]

I was actually thinking about you because the market in [their area] has been [brief update — 'really active' / 'interesting lately']. Your home has probably appreciated quite a bit since you bought — would you want me to run a quick update on what it's worth? Just for your own records, no agenda.

[If yes:] Great, I'll email it over this week.

[Transition to referral:]

Also — if you know anyone who's been thinking about buying or selling, I'd love the chance to help them. You know how I work, and I promise I'd take great care of anyone you send my way.

[If they mention someone:] Oh awesome — would you be comfortable making an introduction? Or I can reach out and mention you sent me. Whatever's easier.

Great talking to you, [Name]. Don't be a stranger!"

## Script 5 — New to the Neighborhood Introduction

**Context:** Door-knocking to introduce yourself as the local agent in a neighborhood you want to farm. No specific listing to reference — you're building presence.

---

"Hi there — I'm [Agent Name] with [Brokerage]. I work specifically in [Neighborhood] and I just wanted to introduce myself. I'm not selling anything today — I'm just getting to know the neighbors and making sure people in the area know there's a local agent who actually lives and breathes this market.

A couple of things I can always help with:

- If you're ever curious about your home's value, I track sales in this area closely and I'm happy to run a quick analysis anytime.
- If you need a contractor, handyman, plumber, or anyone home-related, I have a list of people I personally trust and refer.
- And of course, if you or anyone you know is thinking about buying or selling, I'm here.

[Hand them a card.]

That's it — just wanted to put a face to a name. Have a great [day/evening]!"

## Handling Common Responses

**"I'm not interested."**
"Totally understand. I appreciate your time. If anything ever comes up — even just a question about the market — don't hesitate to reach out. Have a great day."

**"We're not planning to move."**
"That's great — sounds like you love the neighborhood. If you ever get curious about what your home is worth, or if someone you know is looking to move to the area, I'm always happy to help."

**"We already have an agent."**
"That's perfect — glad you're in good hands. If there's ever anything I can do, or if your situation changes, I'm here. Enjoy your [day/evening]."

**"How did you get my number?"**
"Your information was available through [public records / the listing / neighborhood directory]. I understand if you'd prefer not to be contacted — I'll take you off my list. Have a great day."

**"Can you just send me information?"**
"Absolutely — what's the best email? I'll send over [the market analysis / my info / some recent sales data] and you can take a look on your own time. No follow-up unless you want it."

## Tone Notes for Claude

- These scripts should sound like a real person talking, not a call center. Short sentences. Contractions. Natural pauses.
- The agent should practice these out loud before using them. Reading a script sounds like reading a script.
- Always provide an easy exit. "I'm not here to pitch you" and "no obligation" reduce resistance.
- The FSBO and expired scripts require extra sensitivity. These people have either been burned by an agent or are trying to avoid one. Lead with empathy.
- Never badmouth the previous agent (for expired listings). Say "I have some thoughts on what could be done differently" — not "your last agent dropped the ball."
- Adapt for the agent's personality. Some agents are high-energy and fast-talking. Others are quiet and consultative. The words should match who they are.
